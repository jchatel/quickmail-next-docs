# Troubleshooting: SEM Fresh Blacklist

## Why is my domain listed in SEM Fresh?

Newly registered domains may sometimes appear on the SEM Fresh blacklist (`fresh.spameatingmonkey.net` ) shortly after purchase, even if the domain has never been used for sending emails.

This is a common occurrence affecting many newly created `.com`  domains across different providers and is not specific to QuickMail.

## What is SEM Fresh?

SEM Fresh is a temporary blacklist managed by Spam Eating Monkey that tracks newly registered domains.

You can check the status of a domain here:

[https://spameatingmonkey.com/services/SEM-FRESH30](https://spameatingmonkey.com/services/SEM-FRESH30)

According to SEM Fresh, domains may remain listed for approximately 5 to 30 days before being automatically removed.

## Does this impact deliverability?

In most cases, no.

Being listed on SEM Fresh does not necessarily affect:

- Overall domain reputation

- Inbox placement

- Domain warm-up capability

- Email sending performance

Many users are still able to successfully warm up and send emails from domains listed there.

## Recommended action

- Continue warming up and using the domain normally

- No immediate action is typically required

- The listing should automatically expire over time

## Additional notes

This type of listing is considered temporary and is commonly associated with newly purchased domains rather than spam activity.
